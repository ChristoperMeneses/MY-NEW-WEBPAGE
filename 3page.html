Social Behavior...

Recent research has shown that great whites exhibit complex social behavior that establishes rank among individuals. After one great white makes a kill, others sometimes come and feed off the same kill with no apparent aggressive interactions.

Unique Behavior !

Sharks circle their prey, disconcertingly appearing seemingly out of nowhere and frequently approaching from below. Feeding behaviour is stimulated by numbers and rapid swimming when three or more sharks appear in the presence of food. Activity progresses from tight circling to rapid crisscross passes

What does it mean for a shark to swim backawards

Sharks' pectoral fins are placed in a way that stop them producing lift in a backwards motion – so they stick to moving forwards instead. That said, both the nurse shark and the white shark have been shown to move backwards, but it's usually slowly, with great difficult and only ever as a last resort....are capable of moving in reverse to some extent, they typically do not do so with the same agility or speed as they can move forward. Sharks are designed for efficient forward movement, with their streamlined bodies and powerful tails enabling them to propel themselves through the water quickly and with precision. The structure of their fins and the placement of their gills make it more challenging for them to swim backward compared to moving forward. While some sharks may exhibit limited backward movement, it is not a common behavior for them. Their primary mode of swimming is forward, using their powerful tails to propel themselves through the water in pursuit of prey or to navigate their environment. .
